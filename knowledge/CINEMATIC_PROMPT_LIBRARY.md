# Cinematic Prompt Library for Nano Banana Pro & Veo 3.1

This library contains over 40 distinct camera angles, shot sizes, and movements to give you precise control over your AI generations. Use these keywords in your prompts to direct the AI like a cinematographer.

## 1. Distance & Size (The "What")
*Defines how much of the subject and environment is visible.*

| Shot Name | Definition | Primary Purpose | AI Prompt Keyword |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Extreme Wide Shot (EWS)** | Subject is barely visible; emphasizes vast landscape. | Establish vastness, isolation, scale. | `Extreme Wide Shot`, `EWS`, `Vast Landscape` |
| **Wide Shot (WS) / Long Shot** | Full subject (head to toe) + environment. | Context, subject's relationship to surroundings. | `Wide Shot`, `Long Shot`, `Full Body` |
| **Full Shot** | Frames subject head to toe, filling frame. | Show body language and costume clearly. | `Full Shot`, `Head to Toe Framing` |
| **Medium Long Shot (MLS)** | Knees up. (aka "3/4 Shot"). | Focus on subject + some context. | `Medium Long Shot`, `MLS`, `Three-Quarter Shot` |
| **Cowboy Shot** | Mid-thighs up. | Powerful, confident, action-ready. | `Cowboy Shot`, `Mid-Thighs Up` |
| **Medium Shot (MS)** | Waist up. Standard storytelling shot. | Body language + face. Ideal for dialogue. | `Medium Shot`, `Waist Up` |
| **Medium Close-Up (MCU)** | Chest/Shoulders up. | Face focus with some context. | `Medium Close-Up`, `MCU`, `Chest Up` |
| **Close-Up (CU)** | Head and neck only. | Pure emotion, high intimacy. | `Close-Up`, `Face Shot` |
| **Extreme Close-Up (ECU)** | Specific detail (eyes, mouth, finger). | High intensity, critical detail. | `Extreme Close-Up`, `ECU`, `Macro Eyes` |
| **Macro Shot** | Microscopic detail (fabric texture, bug). | Reveal unseen textures. | `Macro Shot`, `Microscopic Detail` |

## 2. Angles (The "Perspective")
*Defines the camera's relationship to the subject.*

| Angle Name | Definition | Primary Purpose | AI Prompt Keyword |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Eye-Level** | Neutral perspective. | Realism, connection, natural view. | `Eye Level`, `Neutral Angle` |
| **Low Angle** | Camera looks **up** at subject. | Power, dominance, scary, heroic. | `Low Angle`, `Looking Up` |
| **High Angle** | Camera looks **down** at subject. | Vulnerability, weakness, smallness. | `High Angle`, `Looking Down` |
| **Overhead / Bird’s Eye** | Straight down (90°) from sky. | Graphic, map-like, scale. | `Bird's Eye View`, `Top Down`, `Overhead` |
| **Worm’s Eye View** | Extremely low, from ground level. | Exaggerated height, unusual perspective. | `Worm's Eye View`, `Ground Level` |
| **Dutch Angle** | Tilted horizon. | Unease, disorientation, chaos, tension. | `Dutch Angle`, `Canted Angle`, `Tilted` |
| **Over-the-Shoulder (OTS)** | Looking past someone's shoulder. | Dialogue, grounding conversation. | `Over-the-Shoulder`, `OTS` |
| **Point of View (POV)** | Camera *is* the character's eyes. | Immersion, first-person experience. | `POV`, `First Person Perspective` |
| **Selfie Angle** | High angle, arm's length. | Social media aesthetic, informal. | `Selfie Angle`, `Arm's Length` |
| **Ground Level** | Camera on floor/surface. | Immersive close-up of environment. | `Ground Level`, `Surface View` |

## 3. Basic Camera Moves (The "Motion")
*Defines how the camera moves through space (Best for Veo 3.1).*

| Move Name | Definition | Primary Purpose | AI Prompt Keyword |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Static** | No movement. Locked off. | Focus on subtle animation/internal conflict. | `Static Shot`, `Tripod Shot` |
| **Pan (Left/Right)** | Camera rotates horizontally on axis. | Follow subject, reveal new scene part. | `Pan Left`, `Pan Right` |
| **Tilt (Up/Down)** | Camera rotates vertically on axis. | Reveal height or vertical structure. | `Tilt Up`, `Tilt Down` |
| **Zoom In** | Lens focal length changes (closer). | Focus attention, flatten background. | `Zoom In`, `Slow Zoom` |
| **Zoom Out** | Lens focal length changes (wider). | Reveal context, shift to environment. | `Zoom Out`, `Pull Back` |
| **Pedestal (Up/Down)** | Camera physically moves up/down. | Change relationship to ground/objects. | `Pedestal Up`, `Pedestal Down` |
| **Dolly (In/Out)** | Camera physically moves toward/away. | Immersion. Background parallax changes (unlike zoom). | `Dolly In`, `Dolly Out` |
| **Truck (Left/Right)** | Camera physically moves sideways. | Follow subject walking, scan a scene. | `Truck Left`, `Truck Right` |

## 4. Advanced Cinematic Moves (The "Director's Cut")
*Complex movements for high-end video generation.*

| Move Name | Definition | Primary Purpose | AI Prompt Keyword |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Arc Shot / Orbit** | Camera circles around the subject. | Show subject from all sides, heroic/dramatic. | `Arc Shot`, `360 Orbit`, `Camera Circles Subject` |
| **Tracking Shot** | Camera moves with the subject (behind/front/side). | Keeps subject anchored while world moves. | `Tracking Shot`, `Follow Shot` |
| **Crane / Jib Shot** | Camera sweeps up and away (or down and in). | Epic intros/outros, establishing scale. | `Crane Shot`, `Jib Up`, `Sweeping Shot` |
| **Rack Focus** | Focus shifts from foreground to background (or vice versa). | Direct audience attention, reveal connection. | `Rack Focus`, `Pull Focus` |
| **Dolly Zoom (Vertigo)** | Dolly in + Zoom out (or vice versa). | Disorienting, psychedelic, realization moment. | `Dolly Zoom`, `Vertigo Effect` |
| **Handheld / Shaky Cam** | Unstable, organic movement. | Realism, chaos, action, documentary vibe. | `Handheld Camera`, `Shaky Cam` |
| **Crash Zoom** | Rapid, sudden zoom in. | Comedic effect, shock, sudden realization. | `Crash Zoom`, `Snap Zoom` |
| **Whip Pan** | Fast pan that blurs the image. | Transition between scenes, high energy. | `Whip Pan`, `Fast Pan` |
| **Roll** | Camera rotates on the lens axis (spinning). | Disorientation, space/underwater vibes. | `Camera Roll`, `Spinning Shot` |
